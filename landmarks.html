LANDMARKS

The Colosseum in Italy, located in the heart of ancient Rome, is one of the world’s most iconic architectural and historical landmarks. Completed in 80 AD, this massive amphitheater could hold tens of thousands of spectators who gathered to watch gladiator battles, theatrical performances, and public spectacles. Built from stone and concrete, its impressive arches and tiered seating showcase the engineering skill of the Roman Empire. Today, the Colosseum stands as a powerful reminder of Rome’s rich history and attracts millions of visitors each year, who come to admire its grandeur and learn about the culture that shaped it.

The Tower of Pisa, famously known as the Leaning Tower of Pisa, is one of Italy’s most recognizable landmarks. Located in the city of Pisa, it was built as the freestanding bell tower for the nearby cathedral. Construction began in 1173, but as the tower rose, the soft, unstable soil beneath it caused it to lean noticeably to one side. Despite this unintentional tilt, the tower remained standing and became an architectural marvel admired around the world. Today, thanks to modern stabilization efforts, visitors can safely climb its spiraling staircase to enjoy stunning views of Pisa, while the tower continues to charm people with its unique and iconic tilt.

St. Peter’s Basilica, located in Vatican City within the city of Rome, Italy, is one of the most important and breathtaking churches in the world. Built over the traditional burial site of Saint Peter, one of Jesus’s apostles, the basilica is a masterpiece of Renaissance architecture. Its construction brought together some of history’s greatest artists and architects, including Michelangelo, who designed the magnificent dome, and Bernini, who shaped the grand St. Peter’s Square. Inside, the basilica is filled with stunning artwork, marble columns, and towering sculptures that reflect centuries of religious devotion and artistic achievement. Today, it remains a major pilgrimage site in Italy and an enduring symbol of faith, beauty, and architectural brilliance.
